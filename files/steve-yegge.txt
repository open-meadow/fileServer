We&#39;re all too busy to do things we don&#39;t want to do. However, you already do a certain amount of writing as part of your job. You write a lot of email, for starters. You also write documentation sometimes — maybe not often, but even if you only produce a document once or twice a year, you&#39;ve still created something people might find useful. And you may also simply take notes for yourself as you&#39;re figuring something out.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">The important thing to realize here is that you can save work by blogging. Many people have figured out that this is true of Wiki: rather than explaining the same thing over and over, you put your explanation in Wiki once, and you&#39;re done. From then on you can just point people to it.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">But Wiki doesn&#39;t feel like the right medium for the kinds of things that go into blogs. And there&#39;s certainly some overlap between what people put in the two, because blogs and wikis are (today) the primary mechanisms for persistent communication, by virtue of having the lowest friction. But for some things you write, your blog is clearly the right venue.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Some of the stuff you write as part of your ordinary workday will be interesting and useful to others. All you need to do is keep an eye out for things you&#39;ve written that might be worth publishing. Then the &quot;I&#39;m too busy&quot; argument just evaporates, because it&#39;s almost no effort to dump some document or email rant or whatever into your blog.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">And it can be about anything at all. I think people recognize that a blog is diary-like, and that we&#39;re all busy doing other things most of the time. People aren&#39;t going to hold you to some exacting standard; they&#39;re not going to demand that every blog entry you write be interesting or useful. Nobody can insist that you blog with regularity — blogs aren&#39;t bowel movements, although certainly some of my entries have shared a certain family resemblance to them. But people are pretty forgiving, on the whole.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Too busy just doesn&#39;t cut it, as far as I&#39;m concerned. You&#39;re not too busy to put one thing a year in your blog, and even that is better than nothing. There&#39;s no law that says you have to keep blogging once you&#39;ve started.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q"><em>Reason #2: I&#39;m afraid to put my true thoughts on public record.</em></p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Yeah, me too. My blog is definitely a watered-down version.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">I know how you feel, even though it might seem to the untrained eye that all I do is drink a bunch of wine, lose all my inhibitions, and bare my soul to the world. It might even seem that way to the trained eye. Well, OK, that <em>is</em> what I do.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">But you don&#39;t need to put your opinions into your blog. You&#39;re free to maintain careful neutrality, straddling the bland fence of noncommital objectivity, positioning yourself as broad-minded yet aloof. Your blog will totally suck, of course, but hey, it&#39;s your blog.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">If you want people to read it, then be yourself. If you think of yourself as an important evangelist for the technological advances in your area, then you&#39;re welcome to write press releases in your blog. Not everyone is going to think like you, but I assure you that <em>some</em> people think just like you do, and they&#39;ll be interested in the things you feel most compelled to talk about.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">That&#39;s a key thing to realize about blogging: you can&#39;t please everyone, and you <em>won&#39;t</em> please everyone, so focus on making yourself happy. I didn&#39;t get many of the details, and I&#39;m not sure if I can share the ones he did tell me, except that it sounded pretty cool, and several giant brains at Amazon are regular attendees.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Duncan (his full name is James Duncan Davidson) was saying that at this convention, everyone is so modest that there&#39;s sometimes a sort of ad-hoc competition to downplay yourself. Someone famous will say they&#39;re amazed to be there, since they&#39;re the dumbest person in the room, and someone else will earnestly say no, <em>I</em> am the dumbest person in the room, really, you don&#39;t get it, I&#39;m the dumbest. Pretty soon everyone wants to be the dumbest, and they vie against their brilliant peers to make the most eloquent case for being the dumbest one in the room. It&#39;s the kind of paradoxical competition only a geek could love.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">There was a lot of crossfire-conversation going on at this dinner I was at, so I didn&#39;t catch whether they gave out some sort of formal award to the person who won the &quot;I&#39;m the dumbest&quot; competition. I wouldn&#39;t know whether to be happy or sad about winning. I imagine the competition sort of peters out before anyone too hammered tries too hard to win it.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Duncan&#39;s point was that the smartest people don&#39;t <em>feel</em> very smart, and the cool ones check their egos at the door.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">If you feel, as I do, that bloggers run the risk of seeming narcissistic, it doesn&#39;t mean you shouldn&#39;t write blogs! Just take the high road, don&#39;t be narcissistic, and hopefully you won&#39;t come across that way.</p><div id="h.p_ID_257" class="GV3q8e aP9Z7e"></div><h3 id="h.p_ID_257_l" dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q OmQG5e" tabindex="-1"><div jscontroller="Ae65rd" jsaction="touchstart:UrsOsc; click:KjsqPd; focusout:QZoaZ; mouseover:y0pDld; mouseout:dq0hvd;fv1Rjc:jbFSOd;CrfLRd:SzACGe;" class="CjVfdc"><div class="PPhIP rviiZ" jsname="haAclf"><div role="presentation" class="U26fgb mUbCce fKz7Od LRAOtb Znu9nd M9Bg4d" jscontroller="mxS5xe" jsaction="click:cOuCgd; mousedown:UX7yZ; mouseup:lbsD7e; mouseenter:tfO1Yc; mouseleave:JywGue; focus:AHmuwe; blur:O22p3e; contextmenu:mg9Pef;" jsshadow aria-describedby="h.p_ID_257_l" aria-label="Copy heading link" aria-disabled="false" data-tooltip="Copy heading link" aria-hidden="true" data-tooltip-position="top" data-tooltip-vertical-offset="12" data-tooltip-horizontal-offset="0"><a class="FKF6mc TpQm9d" href="#h.p_ID_257" aria-label="Copy heading link" jsname="hiK3ld" role="button" aria-describedby="h.p_ID_257_l"><div class="VTBa7b MbhUzd" jsname="ksKsZd"></div><span jsslot class="xjKiLb"><span class="Ce1Y1c" style="top: -11px"><svg class="OUGEr QdAdhf" width="22px" height="22px" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="currentColor" focusable="false"><path d="M0 0h24v24H0z" fill="none"/><path d="M3.9 12c0-1.71 1.39-3.1 3.1-3.1h4V7H7c-2.76 0-5 2.24-5 5s2.24 5 5 5h4v-1.9H7c-1.71 0-3.1-1.39-3.1-3.1zM8 13h8v-2H8v2zm9-6h-4v1.9h4c1.71 0 3.1 1.39 3.1 3.1s-1.39 3.1-3.1 3.1h-4V17h4c2.76 0 5-2.24 5-5s-2.24-5-5-5z"/></svg></span></span></a></div></div>My own blogging</div></h3><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">As it turns out, I write a great deal more than I publish.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">People sometimes express amazement that I can write so much (&quot;so much crap&quot;, I read between their lines). I don&#39;t actually spend much time writing blogs: three hours a week, maybe. I can&#39;t vouch for my quality, but the quantity is certainly there. I attribute my blog-spew ability <em>entirely</em> to my current level of proficiency with Emacs. It&#39;s something that you have to witness for yourself to understand, though. Emacs is to Eclipse as a light saber is to a blaster — but a blaster is a lot easier for anyone to pick up and use. I&#39;m hoping to elaborate on this in a <span class=" aw5Odc" style="text-decoration: underline;"><a class="XqQF9c" href="http://steve.yegge.googlepages.com/effective-emacs.html" target="_blank">future blog</a></span>.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">I don&#39;t publish most of my blogs for various reasons that I&#39;d like to share with you, because if you ever start blogging seriously, I&#39;m sure you&#39;ll run into the same problems.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">For one thing, blogging is a bit weird in that you&#39;re potentially writing for multiple audiences. It can really tie you up in knots. I&#39;ve had people in HR and other non-IT departments come by and tell me that they read my blogs, and I can tell you I wish they hadn&#39;t. The more people who tell you they read your blogs, the more you&#39;ll be tempted to try to make them all happy, and you&#39;ll go completely insane if you try too hard, because it&#39;s impossible.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Blogging is also weird because, as it happens, the best things to write about are things you already know, or have just figured out for yourself. You&#39;d be amazed at how many things you take for granted as &quot;common knowledge&quot; are actually brand new to other smart people. There&#39;s simply too much to know in this world, and we&#39;re all continually learning. (I hope).</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Often I&#39;ll get discouraged because I feel like I&#39;m writing about things that have already been discussed into the ground by others. The thing I have to remember is that there&#39;s a &quot;right time&quot; to learn something, and it&#39;s different for everyone. For example, many universities teach the Scheme programming language to undergrads, in the sort of desperate hope that the students will understand why they&#39;re learning it. Most of them don&#39;t, and I was no exception. Some people don&#39;t get it for years, and some people <em>never</em> get it. I had to spend almost two decades writing over a million lines of production code in twenty-odd programming languages before I finally got it. It just wasn&#39;t &quot;time&quot; for me yet. Now understanding Scheme and its peers are my own personal quest, but I shouldn&#39;t expect it to be that way for everyone.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">That doesn&#39;t mean they&#39;re not interested in hearing about it, though. No matter where you are in your education, some people will be interested in hearing your struggles.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">This is an important thing to keep in mind when you&#39;re blogging. Each person in your audience is on a different clock, and all of them are ahead of you in some ways and behind you in others. The point of blogging is that we all agree to share where we&#39;re at, and not poke fun at people who seem to be behind us, because they may know other things that we won&#39;t truly understand for years, if ever.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">The last big problem I grapple with is biting off too much for a single blog. I find that if I can write a blog in a single sitting, it&#39;ll usually seem worth publishing, at least at the time. Maybe that&#39;s just where I&#39;m at in my growth path as a writer. If I can&#39;t write it in one sitting, I feel like I don&#39;t have something concrete enough to say. But I hope I can grow beyond that, since writing a book (or anything really timeless) is going to require the ability to maintain focus across weeks, months, years. I can only do that with coding, not writing. Someday, maybe.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Anyway, my next few blog entries are going to be attempts to dust off my unfinished essays and see if I can make some of them work. I think I just need to set my standards a bit lower, and just get the dang things out there. This isn&#39;t exactly the communications of the ACM, after all.</p><div id="h.p_ID_279" class="GV3q8e aP9Z7e"></div><h3 id="h.p_ID_279_l" dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q OmQG5e" tabindex="-1"><div jscontroller="Ae65rd" jsaction="touchstart:UrsOsc; click:KjsqPd; focusout:QZoaZ; mouseover:y0pDld; mouseout:dq0hvd;fv1Rjc:jbFSOd;CrfLRd:SzACGe;" class="CjVfdc"><div class="PPhIP rviiZ" jsname="haAclf"><div role="presentation" class="U26fgb mUbCce fKz7Od LRAOtb Znu9nd M9Bg4d" jscontroller="mxS5xe" jsaction="click:cOuCgd; mousedown:UX7yZ; mouseup:lbsD7e; mouseenter:tfO1Yc; mouseleave:JywGue; focus:AHmuwe; blur:O22p3e; contextmenu:mg9Pef;" jsshadow aria-describedby="h.p_ID_279_l" aria-label="Copy heading link" aria-disabled="false" data-tooltip="Copy heading link" aria-hidden="true" data-tooltip-position="top" data-tooltip-vertical-offset="12" data-tooltip-horizontal-offset="0"><a class="FKF6mc TpQm9d" href="#h.p_ID_279" aria-label="Copy heading link" jsname="hiK3ld" role="button" aria-describedby="h.p_ID_279_l"><div class="VTBa7b MbhUzd" jsname="ksKsZd"></div><span jsslot class="xjKiLb"><span class="Ce1Y1c" style="top: -11px"><svg class="OUGEr QdAdhf" width="22px" height="22px" viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="currentColor" focusable="false"><path d="M0 0h24v24H0z" fill="none"/><path d="M3.9 12c0-1.71 1.39-3.1 3.1-3.1h4V7H7c-2.76 0-5 2.24-5 5s2.24 5 5 5h4v-1.9H7c-1.71 0-3.1-1.39-3.1-3.1zM8 13h8v-2H8v2zm9-6h-4v1.9h4c1.71 0 3.1 1.39 3.1 3.1s-1.39 3.1-3.1 3.1h-4V17h4c2.76 0 5-2.24 5-5s-2.24-5-5-5z"/></svg></span></span></a></div></div>I&#39;d love to read your blog</div></h3><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">When it comes down to it, I&#39;m asking you to write blogs because I know you&#39;ve got really interesting things to say, even if you don&#39;t think they&#39;re that interesting. Your life is interesting, and your opinions of technology, Amazon, and life in general matter to me, and to others. I bet you&#39;ve got a lot you could teach me, even if you don&#39;t think you do. Heck, I was in my mid-twenties when I realized I had a gross conceptual misunderstanding about the reason it&#39;s hot in the summer and cold in the winter. The stuff I think I know is an invisible speck compared to the superset of what all people know today. Do me a favor and save me the effort of tracking you down in the hall and asking you to enlighten me.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q">Besides, I&#39;m not ready to hear what you have to say yet. When I <em>am</em> ready, it&#39;ll be in your blog.</p><p dir="ltr" class="CDt4Ke zfr3Q"><em>(Published Jan 23, 2005)</em></p></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></section><section
